Quick Tips on Food Choices:

  • Swap one meal a week from red meat to chicken, or vegetarian
  • Purchase food in right measures to reduce food wastage
  • Alternate foods on the right side to the left side of the charts
  • Choose sustainably farmed food items over organic and local

MEAT & EGG

  • Meat and egg is a food category which creates high amounts of carbon footprint.
  • Reducing meat consumption on a weekly basis has a much greater impact than eating locally sourced meat.
  • Try substituting red meat with white meat and fish and seafood.

DAIRY

Dairy products produced from animals have high levels of carbon footprint compared to non-animal-based dairy products.

  • Try experimenting with plant-based milk and dairy products into your diet to find the one substitute suitable to you.
  • Try dairy products on the left side of the chart over the ones on the right side.

FISH & SEAFOOD

Seafood and freshwater food are a great alternative to meat-eaters over the red meats.

  • Seafood can be chosen over meat during special occasions to balance out your budget and your carbon footprint.
  • Some seafood is better than others due to their farming practices.
  • Our chart takes this into consideration by listing out different kinds of seafood by their carbon footprint.

VEGETABLES

Producing vegetables requires relatively less amount of resources which is reflected in its low carbon footprint.

  • Going sustainable is always better than going organic
  • Growing local foods, or growing them yourself can improve your carbon footprint greatly
  • Knowing where your vegetables come from, and knowing how they were produced can inform you of its sustainability.

FRUITS

Fruits are a great source of nutrients, vitamins and minerals. Fortunately, they have a relatively low carbon footprint.

  • Look for fruits that are grown sustainably (not in hothouses) over organically.
  • Consume fruits that are in season. This will automatically translate to lowering your carbon footprint.

GRAINS

Grains and pulses are the ultimate food items when it comes to the carbon footprint.

  • Grains and pulses can be the base of your dishes at least once or twice a week.
  • Farming practices with grains take relatively less water and other resources.
  • They are a rich source of proteins and fatty acids.
  • Plant-based dairy from these food items is also great for lactose intolerant diets.
